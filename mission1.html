💡 Intelligence Report
Our cryptanalysts believe ShadowNet is using ROT13 encryption - a substitution cipher that replaces each letter with the letter 13 positions ahead in the alphabet. For example: A becomes N, B becomes O, and so on. Letters N-Z wrap around to the beginning of the alphabet.

Fun Fact: ROT13 is its own inverse - applying ROT13 twice returns the original text!
